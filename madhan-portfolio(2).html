02.

How I think about marketing

It's not about what
we're proud of —
it's about what
they need to hear.

Learning has always been the constant for me — and it's not going anywhere. Challenges, pushing limits, solving problems. But most importantly, failure teaches more than success ever could. Every campaign that didn't land taught me something a winning one never would have.

When it comes to marketing, it starts with observing people — how they behave, what they respond to, what actually moves them. And then being willing to set aside your own preferences and act on what you see. Marketing that works is rarely marketing that the team is in love with. It's marketing that the customer recognises themselves in.

Every piece of communication — an ad copy, a billboard, a product video, a landing page — has one job: answer two questions before the customer even asks them. How is this different from everything else out there? And does it actually solve my problem? If the answer to both isn't clear in the first few seconds, you've already lost them.
